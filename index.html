Scope

Details of Camp

The goal of Mind & Body Summer Camp is to provide a platform for Nigerian youth to explore various career paths through an intense four-day enrichment program, which entails personalized mentorship, team building activities, and insightful talks.

The camp targets students who have just completed their Junior Secondary School education. The transition from Junior to Secondary School in the Nigerian education is one that can be made smoother and at the same time, more productive for students.

This camp is designed to stimulate and encourage these students to critically consider multiple career paths that appeal to them by introducing various STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics) career possibilities through workshops. Additionally, the informal peer mentor relationships will enable students to engage in productive conversations with their respective mentors and establish goals they can work towards together. It will also provide these students with the opportunity to freely voice their opinions and get immediate feedback from mentors on their communication and presentation skills.

Eligibility: Preference will be given to students who have completed their Junior Secondary School however, all secondary school students are strongly encouraged to apply.

Talks

Invited Speakers

During the program, there will also be a STEM career speaker series. The speakers will either be one of the peer mentors available at the camp or professional individuals brought in to give a presentation about the specific details of their line of work and their academic and professional trajectories.

The vision for this is to educate and enlighten the students about experiences at different STEM career levels and also to create a network with professional mentors.

Camp Speakers will be listed on this page once they are confirmed.

Activities

Mentorship and Extracurriculars

This camp will employ university students and recent graduates, who are academically brilliant and passionate about education. They will serve as peer mentors and instructors because they are closer in age to the campers. The reason for this is to break any potential fear barrier that might prevent these young students from utilizing traditional mentors, who are much older and imposing, as resources.

The peer mentors will also facilitate the interaction with guest speakers from different lines of work and help students figure out what future career will best suit their individual skills and interests. The small student to peer mentor ratio (6:1) will be beneficial for students to build strong and lifelong relationships with their mentors.

Students will also be given the opportunity to engage in various sporting activities everyday in order to foster development of the mind and body, a two fold well rounded process, which is necessary for efficient learning and interaction. This physical component will also encourage students to learn how to work as a team and serve as planned recreation for the students.

Ebuka Arinze

Founder, CEO and President

Ebuka was born in Lagos, Nigeria where he spent the first 17 years of his life. He started out at Nazareth Nursery and Primary school in Festac Town, Lagos then he proceeded to attend Loyola Jesuit College in Abuja for his secondary school education. After this foundation of education, in 2009, Ebuka enrolled at Fairfield University, Connecticut where he studied Electrical Engineering and graduated in the spring of 2013 with a B.S in Electrical Engineering.

It was from witnessing the various summer camp programs that took place at Fairfield University that Ebuka was inspired to develop a program for the Nigerian youth where they can get a direct insight into the personal experiences of various STEM professionals. While at Fairfield University, he also spent time working in the Operations Division of UBS at Stamford, CT. In the fall of 2013, Ebuka joined the Electrical and Computer Engineering department at Johns Hopkins University and is currently a graduate student pursuing his Ph.D. Degree. His on-going work focuses on third generation solar cells and his research interests include photovoltaics and energy storage devices, integrated photonics, photonic metamaterials, and nanotechnology.

Nonso Anyigbo

COO and Vice President

After attending Loyola Jesuit college, Nonso Anyigbo enrolled in Rice University as an electrical and computer engineering major. From his sophomore year he served as an academic advisor of Mathematics and Physics courses. In that capacity, he tutored students on various freshman STEM courses and hosted weekly review sessions.

In his final year at Rice, he briefly took up a job tutoring high school students around Houston in various STEM courses. His experience tutoring students has motivated him to embark on this journey to eduacate and empower children in Nigeria. Nonso currently works as an electrical engineer for GE Oil & Gas. He is also pursuing his MSc in electrical engineering from Georgia Institute of Technology.

Tayo Odunlami

CMO and Creative Director

Tayo Odunlami is from Ogun State, Nigeria. He completed his Secondary education in Nigeria before moving to the US to study Architecture. He holds a B.A. in Architecture with a focus on structural engineering from the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign. His interest in architecture came at an early age, from observing his environment and hoping to improve on the infrastructure surrounding him. As a result he began hand sketching simple solutions to design problems he encountered. While in high school, he attended a BIM modeling workshop. At this point he was able to shift from hand sketches to fully conceptualized compositions.

He currently works for Cannon Design, a global design firm that unites architects, engineers and planners. He is very passionate about design, believing that it should be both beautiful and functional. As an active professional in the field of design and engineering he has learned that creativity is an essential factor in making projects in any field successful. He hopes to contribute his technical knowledge, and also encourage the exploration of creativity and self-expression within the students.
